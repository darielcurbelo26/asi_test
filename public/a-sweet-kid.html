A Sweet Kid

Mexico City Art Week 2025
Salon Silicon

A Sweet Kid was a multimedia installation that explored the fractured reality of living a double life. The 6' x 8' sterile padded space replicated an institutional solitary confinement cell with short film script pages lining the exterior to give visitors narrative context before entering.

Inside, they experienced a 13-minute spatial audio journey of evolving negative self-talk alongside commissioned paintings by artist Shelby Keierleber that explored fragmented identity.

This marked ISEEASI's first installation and spatial audio experience, building on her previous work examining personal trauma and social masks. The environment created a space where visitors could confront their own experiences of authenticity and performance in daily life.
